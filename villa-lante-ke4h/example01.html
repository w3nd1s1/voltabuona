Villa Lante di Bagnaia – Conservation Intervention Enrichment

Background

Conservation interventions are essential for documenting the life cycle of cultural heritage assets. Recording restoration activities improves the historical documentation of a monument, supports conservation management, and provides information about preservation strategies and funding programmes. Within Cultural Heritage Knowledge Graphs, these interventions represent valuable contextual information that complements traditional cataloguing metadata.

Task

Create a SPARQL CONSTRUCT query that generates proposed RDF triples for enriching ArCo with information about a recent conservation intervention involving Villa Lante di Bagnaia.

Important Context

  • We are not directly modifying the public ArCo endpoint.
  • The endpoint is used exclusively to verify the identified knowledge gap and generate proposed enrichment triples.
  • Do not use INSERT DATA.
  • Use SPARQL CONSTRUCT because it returns RDF triples representing the proposed enrichment.
  • The query must generate only enrichment triples and must not modify the existing ArCo resource.

Identified Gap

The ArCo record provides historical and descriptive information about Villa Lante but does not include the most recent conservation intervention funded by the Italian National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR). This project, launched in 2024, represents one of the most significant restoration campaigns carried out on the monument in recent years.

Missing information:

  • Current conservation intervention (2024–2026).
  • Funding programme (Italian National Recovery and Resilience Plan – PNRR).
  • Restoration of the historical fountains and formal gardens.
  • Reconstruction of the hydraulic network.
  • Reactivation of the historical water features.
  • Flora and fauna survey of the park.
  • Census of approximately 600 trees.

Target Resource

Subject IRI

External Sources

  • Official website of Villa Lante di Bagnaia.
  • Italian Ministry of Culture.
  • Official PNRR documentation concerning the restoration project.

Requirements

  • Use SPARQL CONSTRUCT, not INSERT DATA.
  • Verify that the target resource exists by checking its rdfs:label.
  • Generate RDF triples only for the existing ArCo cultural property.
  • Do not create a new cultural property resource.
  • Represent the intervention as a ConservationIntervention.
  • Associate the intervention with Villa Lante using the appropriate ArCo properties.
  • Represent PNRR as the activity sponsor.
  • Include both rdfs:label and rdfs:comment describing the intervention.
  • Follow Linked Open Data and Cultural Heritage best practices.

Expected Enrichment

  1. Preserve the existing ArCo resource representing Villa Lante.
  2. Create a new resource describing the conservation intervention.
  3. Link the intervention to the cultural property.
  4. Associate the intervention with its funding programme (PNRR).
  5. Provide a semantic description of the restoration activities.
  6. Improve the documentation of Villa Lante's recent conservation history.
